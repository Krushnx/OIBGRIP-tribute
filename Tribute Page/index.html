Master Blaster Sachin Tendulkar: A Tribute to the God of Cricket

Sachin Tendulkar, is a name that needs no introduction. He is one of the greatest cricketers to have ever played the sport and is often considered the greatest batsman of all time. Sachin is a legend in his own right and has achieved numerous records and accolades throughout his career. Born in Mumbai on April 24, 1973, Sachin started playing cricket at a very young age. He made his debut for the Indian cricket team in 1989, at the age of 16, and soon established himself as a prodigious talent. Sachin's journey in cricket has been nothing short of phenomenal. He has achieved numerous records and accolades throughout his career, making him one of the most decorated cricketers of all time.
      Sachin's greatest asset as a batsman was his technique. He had a textbook technique and was known for his impeccable timing and placement. He was equally comfortable against pace and spin, and could play both attacking and defensive strokes with equal ease. Sachin's ability to play under pressure was another one of his strengths. He had an uncanny ability to rise to the occasion when it mattered the most and had a reputation of being a big-match player. Sachin's records in cricket are nothing short of incredible. He is the only player in the history of the sport to have scored 100 international centuries, which is a feat that is unlikely to be matched anytime soon. Sachin also holds the record for the most number of runs scored in international cricket, with a staggering 34,357 runs to his name. He has also scored the most number of runs in both Test and ODI cricket. Apart from his batting prowess, Sachin was also an excellent fielder and a useful bowler. He could bowl both spin and medium-pace and had a knack for picking up wickets at crucial junctures in the game. Sachin's contribution to Indian cricket is immeasurable. He is a true ambassador of the sport and has inspired a whole generation of cricketers in India and around the world.





Awards

  • 1988 - Polly Umrigar Award for Best Junior Cricketer of the Year
  • 1994 - Arjuna Award for Outstanding Performance in Cricket
  • 1998 - Wisden Cricketer of the Year
  • 1999 - Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, India's highest sporting honor
  • 2001 - Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award
  • 2008 - Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award
  • 2010 - ICC Cricketer of the Year and Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for Cricketer of the Decade
  • 2014 - Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award

World Records

  • Most runs in international cricket : 34,357
  • Most centuries in international cricket : 100
  • Most runs in World Cup history : 2,278
  • Most runs in a single edition of World Cup : 673
  • Most centuries in World Cup history : 6
  • Most runs in Test cricket : 15,921
  • Most centuries in Test cricket : 51
  • Most centuries against a single team in Test cricket : 9 vs Australia
  • Most Man of the Match awards in international cricket : 62
  • Most Man of the Series awards in international cricket : 15
